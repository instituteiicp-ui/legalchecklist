Part 1: Monthly Compliance Checklist

Compliance Area Due Date Document Required Penalty for Non-Compliance
Employees' Provident Fund (PF) 15th of each month ECR (Electronic Challan cum Return), Challan ‚Çπ1,000/month + 12% interest per annum on delayed payments
Employees' State Insurance (ESI) 15th of each month ESIC Return, Contribution Sheet Up to ‚Çπ18,000 fine + imprisonment up to 2 years
Professional Tax (PT) State-specific (Usually 15th-25th) PT Challan, Monthly Return As per State Act (Fines + interest on delay)
TDS on Salary 7th of next month Form 24Q, Challan 281 Interest @1% per month + penalty under Income Tax Act
LWF (Labour Welfare Fund) State-specific dates LWF Challan, Return As per State Welfare Fund Act

üìù Monthly Compliance Best Practices

  1. Calendar Alerts: Set reminders 3 days before each due date
  2. Document Preparation: Prepare challans and returns in advance
  3. Reconciliation: Match employee contributions with company records
  4. Payment Tracking: Maintain payment acknowledgment records
  5. Team Coordination: Ensure payroll, finance, and HR are aligned
üîÑ Monthly Workflow Process
  1. Run payroll calculations (1st-5th of month)
  2. Prepare salary slips and contribution sheets (5th-7th)
  3. Calculate statutory deductions (PF, ESI, PT, TDS) (7th-10th)
  4. Generate challans and make payments (10th-14th)
  5. File returns online (14th-15th)
  6. Maintain acknowledgment records (15th-17th)

Part 4: Statutory Registers & Part 5: Employee Documentation

üìö Mandatory Statutory Registers (Under Various Acts)

1. Register of Wages

Acts: Payment of Wages Act, Minimum Wages Act
Contents: Employee details, wage periods, deductions, net wages paid
Retention: 3 years after last entry

2. Master Roll / Attendance Register

Acts: Factories Act, Shops & Establishments Act
Contents: Daily attendance, overtime hours, weekly offs
Retention: 3 years minimum

3. Leave & Overtime Register

Acts: Factories Act, State Shops Acts
Contents: Leave applied, granted, balance; Overtime calculations
Retention: 3 years minimum

4. Accident Register

Act: Factories Act, Workmen Compensation Act
Contents: Details of workplace accidents, treatment, compensation
Retention: 5 years minimum

5. ESI & PF Contribution Records

Acts: ESI Act, EPF Act
Contents: Monthly contributions, challan details, returns
Retention: 6 years minimum

Part 6: 2024-25 Key Updates & Career Opportunities

üö® Major Labour Law Updates for 2024-25

1. Wage Code Implementation

Change: Basic pay must be minimum 50% of CTC (Cost to Company)
Impact: Higher PF contributions, increased gratuity calculations
Action Required: Restructure salary components in appointment letters

2. ESI Coverage Expansion

Change: Coverage increased up to ‚Çπ21,000 monthly wages (from ‚Çπ15,000)
Impact: More employees eligible for ESI benefits
Action Required: Review employee eligibility and register newly eligible staff

3. PF Auto-Transfer via UAN

Change: Form 13 removed; seamless transfer via UAN portal
Impact: Faster PF transfers when changing jobs
Action Required: Ensure all employees have activated UAN

4. Implementation of 4 Labour Codes

Codes: Wage Code, Industrial Relations Code, Social Security Code, OSH Code
Status: Phased implementation across states
Action Required: Stay updated on state notifications and prepare for changes
